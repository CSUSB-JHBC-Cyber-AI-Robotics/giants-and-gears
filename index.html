CODEX I · ON THE MATHEMATICS OF MACHINES THAT MOVE

Giants & Gears

A mathematics curriculum where every idea is met the way humanity met it: as the answer to a machine that could not yet move, sense, or decide.

the charter

We teach the wrong thing about numbers

School mathematics grades the calculation and discards the question. Students learn that sine is a button, not that it took nineteen centuries, four civilizations, and a stubborn Swiss genius to turn a surveyor's ratio into a function a robot can call a thousand times a second.

In this curriculum every unit obeys five invariants: the robot fails first — no unit opens with a definition; history is load-bearing — the mathematician arrives exactly when their problem matches the robot's; one artifact formula per unit, framed like the museum piece it is; the lab is the assessment — success means the machine works; and everything runs offline, from the portraits to the simulators.
